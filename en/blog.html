December 6, 2023

Reception of the Delegation of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development

 On December 6, 2023, we held a presentation of our projects in front of a delegation from the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD). The event represented a unique opportunity to share our team’s vision and achievements in highly relevant and important fields.
 The presented projects were structured around two main themes: "Smart City" and "Colonizing the Planet Mars". In this context, we transformed the SMART laboratory of the college into a representation of the planet Mars, while the physics laboratory was set up to represent planet Earth. On "planet Mars" we presented the FTC robot and Fable-type robots, which demonstrate our skills in robotics and advanced engineering. These projects highlighted the technological potential necessary for the exploration and colonization of other planets. On "planet Earth" we showcased a wide range of smart solutions for the urban environment. Among our projects were a smart house, a functional elevator with sensors, and mini Lego car-robots with automatic traffic lights.

 In addition, we simulated advanced research centers in fields such as optics, nuclear energy, and medicine, using specific equipment and images. These centers were designed to illustrate the complexity and diversity of scientific research that can contribute to the technological and scientific development of society.

 We are glad we had the opportunity to present our projects in front of such a prestigious audience and that we contributed to promoting the STEM field!

September 2-10, 2023

Winning Alliance - Open Robotics Intelligent Grid

 Between September 6-10, 2023, we participated in the Open Robotics Intelligent Grid camp, organized by the West University of Timișoara in collaboration with the CSH robotics team of the National Pedagogical College "Carmen Sylva".
 Six members, accompanied by a mentor, went to Timișoara to develop their skills and to learn how to build a robot in record time. The main challenge of the camp was to build a functional robot in 48 hours, using only the parts we brought with us from Bucharest. In addition to gaining knowledge about mechanics and programming, we met many robotics teams from all over the country, forming friendships with young people passionate about the same fields as us.

 In the competition finals, we were chosen in an alliance by the Modus Vivendi team, alongside whom we won after a spectacular and emotional match. We returned home with the “Winning Alliance” trophy and with great hopes for the upcoming challenges and the new FTC season.

July 10-21, 2023

Zenith Bootcamp

 In the summer of 2023, the "Zenith" robotics club organized a bootcamp for new members. This training period consisted of two weeks during which students learned basic notions of robotics from mentors or senior members.
 The first week of the bootcamp took place in-person, in the SmartLab of the "Grigore Moisil" National College. It started with an introduction to the FIRST Tech Challenge, and after a lot of theoretical information about robotics, the mechanics department had to build a robot inspired by the PowerPlay season. CAD courses were also held, where students learned to design various parts in Fusion 360, as well as to print them using 3D printers. In addition, the programming department presented several applications created with the help of Arduino boards, while the graphics and PR departments held workshops on social media posts, poster design and contacting sponsors.

 The second week of the bootcamp was held online, and students enjoyed several programming, graphics, and PR sessions, accompanied by discussions based on the GM0 robotics manual.

 They were two wonderful weeks in which, in addition to the technical knowledge gained, the new members had the chance to meet other people who share the same passion as them—robotics.

March 20, 2023

EggDrop Challenge, 2nd Edition

 The EggDrop Challenge is a competition organized by the Zenith Robotics Club at the "Grigore Moisil" National College. It involves creating a device designed to protect an egg, dropped freely from a certain height (the first or second floor).
 In 2023, the second edition of this competition took place, where the creativity and enthusiasm of our colleagues exceeded our expectations. During the event, each team had the opportunity to test their device up to two times, with the best result being taken into account. Thus, the prizes were awarded based on the performance of the devices, the technical notebook (which contained information about the project’s construction process), and the jury’s vote.

 We are happy to promote the applicability of physics and to closely observe the talent and ingenuity of our school’s students.
